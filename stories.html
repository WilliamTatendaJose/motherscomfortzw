The Founder's story and the birth of Mother's Comfort

Becoming and mom has been life transforming for me, my birth experience led me on a journey with a focus on under privileged pregnant women.

I had a very capable Gynaecologist and chose a prestigious birthing centre to ensure I experience a seamless birthing experience, however at 33 weeks of pregnancy I had near-death experience and had an emergency c-section after having a severe case of pregnancy induced hypertension (PIH) chances of survival for both my child and I were reducing however my Gynaecologist and the medical personnel attending to me worked hard to stabilise me. I got post natal care and was in the HDU for a week and my child was in the NNU for about a month

Based on that history of PIH my gynaecologist advised me to get prenatal care on my future pregnancies so as to minimise the risk of developing PIH again, the next two pregnancies, got prenatal care , my gynae and I took the necessary precautions and I ended up having an emergency c-section at 35 weeks on my second pregnancy and 36 weeks on the third pregnancy due to PIH but these c-section was not as high risk as the first one. From that experience I realised how important the role my gynaecologist had played in ensuring that I had a safe pregnancy journey. Without access to this critical and timely care my children and I would not be here today.

Following my experience with prenatal, antenatal and post natal care I then had to put myself in the shoes of those mothers who are unable to have access to antenatal care during to various reasons including not affording registration fees for antenatal care, I realised that some maternal deaths or infant deaths are avoidable if only the mothers had the opportunity to get prenatal, antenatal, and post-natal care.

Many other women are not as fortunate, my story is not exceptional not is it unique, every woman has a story. Hence Motherâ€™s Comfort was born, so as to help improve the quality of life of underprivileged pregnant women.
